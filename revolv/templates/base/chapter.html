Thanks for your interest in becoming a Solar Ambassador! Many of you might be wondering, what does a Solar Champion do? Is this going to be hard? What exactly does this entail? We’ll explain all of that. And in fact we’d love to discuss it in detail with you over the phone or in person sometime.

First let’s start with the why? Why might an individual like you want to lead a solar project for a nonprofit in your community?
A. Do something about climate change and empower your community
People around the country know that it’s time to move off of a fossil fuel economy and onto clean energy. So what are we waiting for? We can all play our role in kickstarting the rooftop revolution. You can play a very special role. Bringing a project to your community that your community can rally behind.
B. Help a local nonprofit
Nonprofits play a critical role in our community. The nonprofit schools, houses of worship, homeless shelters, food kitchens, and community centers play vital roles for so many. With solar energy, we can save these nonprofits 15% or more on their electric bills. This results in thousands of dollars put back into the community to better provide vital services.
C. Raise awareness about solar energy in your community
A little known fact about solar energy is that it’s contagious. In other words, solar energy has a viral effect. In study after study it shows that when someone goes solar their neighbors are more likely to go solar, and their neighbors go solar at faster and faster rates. This means that if we put solar up on a popular place in the community, like the local school, then all the people who interact with the school: the parents, the teachers, the students, will all learn something about solar energy, will become more familiar with it, and the studies suggest that more people will go solar as a result. You can create this catalyst in your community.
D. Create local jobs/economic development
Solar employment has grown by 86% in the past five years alone. One out of every 80 new jobs created in the U.S. since the 2008 recession was created by the solar industry. The solar industry is adding workers at a rate nearly 20 times faster than the overall economy. Could we help bring some of those jobs to our communities? You bet. Solar energy creates local jobs because you can’t outsource the work. A local solar company has to be contracted to work locally and that brings jobs and economic development to your community. By becoming a Solar Champion, you will bring the solar energy revolution to your community and the jobs that come with it.
